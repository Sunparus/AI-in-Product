# 4-Stage Monthly Strategic Pipeline for Executive-Level Decision Making

## Focus Areas
- **Fintech Credit Decisioning**
- **Regulatory Forcing Functions**
- **Product Architecture Changes**

## 1. Stage One: Analysis and Discovery
   - **Objectives**: Assess the current landscape in fintech credit decisioning and identify regulatory changes impacting product architecture.
   - **Input Data Sources**: Market research, regulatory updates, internal performance metrics.
   - **Key Activities**:
     - Conduct SWOT analysis.
     - Gather insights from stakeholders.
   - **Deliverables**:
     - Comprehensive analysis report.
     - Initial risk assessment.

## 2. Stage Two: Strategy Development
   - **Objectives**: Develop strategic responses to identified challenges and opportunities.
   - **Key Activities**:
     - Workshop sessions to brainstorm solutions.
     - Develop strategic framework based on analysis.
   - **Deliverables**:
     - Strategic plan document.
     - Roadmap for implementation.

## 3. Stage Three: Implementation Planning
   - **Objectives**: Create a roadmap to execute the strategies developed in Stage Two.
   - **Key Activities**:
     - Define budget and resource allocation.
     - Identify milestones and KPIs.
   - **Deliverables**:
     - Detailed implementation plan.
     - Presentation to executive team.

## 4. Stage Four: Review and Adaptation
   - **Objectives**: Review execution of strategies and adapt as necessary.
   - **Key Activities**:
     - Collect feedback from stakeholders.
     - Analyze performance against KPIs.
   - **Deliverables**:
     - Review report with recommendations.
     - Adjusted strategic plan for next cycle.

## Search Patterns
- Identify industry trends related to fintech.
- Monitor regulatory changes affecting credit decision models.

## Classification Rules
- Classify insights into strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats.
- Categorize regulatory updates by relevance to product lines.

## Section Assembly Structure
- Each section should clearly state the objectives, key activities, deliverables, and responsible parties.

## Writing Guidelines
- Use clear and concise language.
- Avoid jargon unless defined.
- Keep paragraphs to 4 sentences max.

## 16-Point QA Checklist
1. Is the objective clear and achievable?  
2. Are all key activities defined?  
3. Is each deliverable measurable and relevant?  
4. Is the document free of typos and grammatical errors?  
5. Are terms defined clearly?  
6. Is the structure consistent across sections?  
7. Are stakeholders correctly identified?  
8. Are budget and resources explained?  
9. Are timelines realistic and achievable?  
10. Is feedback incorporated from previous stages?  
11. Is the proposed strategy innovative?  
12. Did the review process effectively assess performance?  
13. Are risks clearly identified and addressed?  
14. Is each section adequately documented?  
15. Are recommendations actionable?  
16. Is the document accessible to all stakeholders?

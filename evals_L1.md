# 5-Dimension QA Framework for Monthly L1 Strategic Issues

This document outlines a 5-dimension QA framework specifically designed to address monthly L1 strategic issues focused on fintech credit decisioning.

## 1. Decision Clarity
- **Criteria**: The strategic decision fork should be actionable and unambiguous.
- **Pass/Fail**: 
  - **Pass**: Clear action items are defined, and decision implications are well-articulated.
  - **Fail**: Vague or ambiguous decision paths without actionable steps.
- **Examples**: 
  - **Pass**: Defined product adjustments based on customer feedback. 
  - **Fail**: A decision that has multiple interpretations leading to confusion.

## 2. Regulatory & Governance Grounding
- **Criteria**: Regulatory/gov implications must be concrete and product-relevant, focusing on:
  - EU AI Act Article 6(2)(b)
  - Aug 2, 2026 deadline for conformity assessment
  - Provider vs. Deployer classification with product implications.
- **Pass/Fail**: 
  - **Pass**: Clear mapping of regulations to product features. 
  - **Fail**: Generic legal summaries without product relevance.
- **Examples**: 
  - **Pass**: Specific regulatory compliance measures tied to product design. 
  - **Fail**: Information that lacks application in product strategy.

## 3. Independence & Vendor Bias
- **Criteria**: Signals should be independent, corroborated by at least one independent source.
- **Pass/Fail**: 
  - **Pass**: At least one independent source confirms findings. 
  - **Fail**: Decisions based solely on vendor claims without external validation.
- **Examples**: 
  - **Pass**: Market research data supporting vendor announcements. 
  - **Fail**: Relying solely on vendor press releases.

## 4. Time Horizon Specificity
- **Criteria**: Timeframes must be explicit and realistic for the conformity assessment scope (4-6 months for high-risk systems).
- **Pass/Fail**: 
  - **Pass**: Timeline is clear and aligns with compliance deadlines. 
  - **Fail**: Undefined or unrealistic timelines.
- **Examples**: 
  - **Pass**: A detailed timeline for product rollouts that align with assessments. 
  - **Fail**: Statements lacking specificity about key milestones.

## 5. Actionability for Product Leaders
- **Criteria**: Product leaders should be able to take immediate actions.
- **Pass/Fail**: 
  - **Pass**: Insights lead to actionable steps within the current month. 
  - **Fail**: Insights that do not prompt any actionable outcomes soon.
- **Examples**: 
  - **Pass**: Clear recommendations for the next month’s product updates. 
  - **Fail**: Recommendations too vague to implement promptly.

## 16-Point QA Checklist for L1 Issues
1. Is the decision fork actionable and unambiguous?
2. Are regulatory implications product-relevant?
3. Are vendor claims corroborated by independent sources?
4. Is the timeframe for conformity assessment realistic?
5. Can immediate actions be taken based on insights?
6. Are action items clearly defined?
7. Is there a timeline that aligns with product goals?
8. Are regulatory summaries concrete and applicable?
9. Are all dimensions assessed to ensure a holistic view?
10. Have potential risks been identified and addressed?
11. Is the report tailored for product leaders?
12. Are key performance indicators outlined? 
13. Are examples provided for clarity?
14. Is there a feedback mechanism in place?
15. Are sources of information clearly cited?
16. Is the document reviewed by multiple stakeholders?
